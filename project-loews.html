Loews Hotels is somewhere in the middle of the reliable hotel chain everyone can trust and the small family-owned boutique hotel where you can expect the unexpected. Loews Hotels asked us for a way to put their portfolio of hotels under one banner that highlighted their unique experiences.

Feel at home, no matter far you’ve traveled

A new look and feel to integrate Loews' unique hotels under the banner of travel and exploration.
The Brief

Each Loews is a different experience

Each Loews hotel is a different experience, with 26 different hotels in 26 different cities, offering up a stay with their own personalities. Loews wanted to highlight the unique experiences that can only be found at each individual Loews, but within the comforts of a hotel chain known for their excellent service.

Our Solution

Wonder.
Far and wide.

We brought to life a spaceman to represent the traveler in all of us, finding adventure in Loews Hotels' unique guest experiences. With our design team we created a new brand tone and laid out user journeys that integrated experiences that would bring wonder to a guest's stay.

My role as art director on Publicis’ pitch team was from start to finish from idea conception to finalized brand messaging, design and experiences. Under the leadership of our Creative Director and Design Lead, my copywriter and I concepted and rebranded Loews Hotels under a new headline, ‘Wonder. Far and wide’. We brought to life a spaceman to represent the traveler in all of us, finding adventure in Loews Hotels' unique guest experiences. With our design team we created a new brand tone and laid out detailed user journeys that integrated experiences we created to bring wonder to a guests' stay.

Understand

Finding the traveler in all of us

We started with secondary research of the the travel industry, utilizing quantitative data of the industry, but also insightful guest reviews from travel websites to help us understand who were most likely to stay at Loews' unique hotels. Our aim was to gather insights about strategies and best practices when it comes to what guests look for in their hotel experiences and in particular the positive feelings that stayed with previous Loews guests.

Primary Persona

Why travel? To get a little lost. See new sights. Feel new thrills.

Name Ariel McCallan

Age & Occupation 28, Designer

Budget $250 per night

Needs

Ariel is a hard-working millenial who works hard to find the right balance between climbing the corporate ladder and working on her own personal growth. She sees travel as important to her own personal growth. It's a way for her to connect with herself while in a new environment. She has used local services like Airbnb in the past because of their price and integration into the local community.

Her hotel budget has grown, but her appreciation for local culture and spontaneous travel hasn't waned one bit. For her next trip she wants to find a hotel that has the comforts of home, but is inspiring and in touch with the community.

Secondary Persona

A business trip doesn't have to be all business.

Name Kevin Robertson

Age & Occupation 38, Accountant

Budget $375 per night

Needs

Kevin is one of many business travelers who are looking to see more than another meeting room while traveling for work. Even if he's only staying for a night, he wants to try local things and feel the local culture.

For a business traveler who doesn't have time to go out and explore hidden local spots, Loews' hotels bring the local culture and wonder to them. According to Forbes, bleisure (business + leisure) travel is up 20% and the most popular cities for bleisure travel happen to be where Loews has a hotel.

Competitive Analysis

Out of this world

A competitive analysis of other hotels would show us not only what features we needed to focus on but also help us define what separates Loews from other hotel chains. Based on each category's strength and pain points, we were able to see what journeys were available now and what direction we wanted to take with Loews.

Boutique.

These hotels attract the most similar crowd that Loews is well-known to. They add personality to their hotel experience and create signature touchpoints throughout the guest’s stay. The Standard's logo for example already conveys to prospective guests that their experience will be something unique.

Global chains

Loews' consistent service and breadth of locations (27 in the US) makes it a reliable and safe choice for travellers looking for a safe hotel brand. Compared to another Hilton or Marriott where you know what to expect, Loews designs and amenities often stand out and draw in the slighlty more adventurous traveler.

Airbnb

Airbnb has disrupted the hospitality industry and given the general public a new yearning for authentic travel experiences. Loews has a unique set of traits and amenities to stand out as the ‘Airbnb’ of quality hotels.

Takeaways from Research

We travel, initially, to lose ourselves.
And we travel, next, to find ourselves.

Exploring a new place can make you feel like you’re on the outside looking in: an alien in a strange land. By intrinsically being part of the neighborhood around them, every Loews hotel stands apart from its competition. And while one visit might not be enough to help you live like a local, every Loews location can make you feel like you belong.

Every Loews hotel is integrated into the fabric of the city.

We should feature the cityscape as Loews’ backyard, showing how the spirit of each Loews hotel is in the fabric of the city.

No two Loews hotels are alike

26 hotels in 26 different locations. Guest reviews show that discovering each location is an adventure itself. From the location, to the employees, to the building itself, no two Loews hotels are alike.

Travelers want to belong

The introduction of services like Airbnb have succeeded because travelers want more personal experience and a sense of community. Let’s make guests feel at home, no matter how far they’ve traveled.

Creating the mood

Wonder.
Far and Wide.

Throughout our pitch we met with our stakeholders on a weekly basis, presenting our ideas and directions in rapid sprints so that we could get feedback to refine, tweak or even scrap ideas. We went back to the drawing board many times, as each round of feedback would keep us learning more and more about Loews. While the most research was done in the early stages to help us understand guests, the company and their unique position in the industry, research was continually needed each time we wanted to incorporate a new idea into the guest’s journey.

Guest Experience

Designed for every touchpoint

Throughout the hotels we would create a series of sensorial touches, both big and small designed to evoke a sense of wonder. From control board sounds to replace elevators dings, to door swooshes that follow our guests as they enter, to space foods upon check in and VR experiences in the lobby, every sensation from sight to smell will be thought out, a reflection of Loews’ dedication to detail and guest relations.

Learnings

Always exploring

It was my first time playing a leading role on such a big project and seeing my initial ideas grow into a campaign that people across the agency were putting their hard-work, sweat and late nights taught me how encouraging and rewarding it was to see a project through with the team you started it with. I'll never forget the faith my creative director put in me and my partner to carry out a little idea into a full campaign for a potential client. At the beginning of the ideation phase, our initial goal of rebranding was to help Loews stand out from a crowded market, but our idea quickly grew into a campaign that would bring all the best that Loews has to offer into a unique experience that could resonate with guests like “our spaceman”.

I learned the effectiveness of quick design sprints. We went back to the drawing board many times, as each round of feedback with our stakeholders would keep us learning more and more about Loews. Research was continually needed each time we wanted to incorporate a new idea into the guest’s journey. From start to finish we were always exploring and open to new things and I believe that’s what made the process so wonderous.
