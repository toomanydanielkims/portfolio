Dialogue in the Dark

Duration
4 weeks
Type
Integrated Campaign
Print / OOH
Tools
Photoshop
Role
Art Director

The dialogue in the dark exhibition is an experience like no other. The exhibitions provide an opportunity for people to learn from blind guides firsthand. You need to rely on your other senses in the pitch darkness. The exhibition aims to change mindsets on disability and diversity, and increase tolerance for “otherness”. I visited the exhibit while in Hong Kong and it profoundly impacted the way I saw the world. For a project in my creative sequence, my partner and I wanted to see how we could create a campaign to bring more exposure to this often misunderstood topic.

As we brainstormed how we could bring awareness to this cause, we realized that we needed to first assess DITD's brand voice. Dialogue in the Dark's mission to increase tolerance and change our views on disability and diversity was what particularly stood out to us.

The more we researched and listened to interviews the more we realized that our tone should convey DITD's message of empowerment. In the end our messaging wasn't complicated, we simply wrote facts out in plain sight, but with tinted black text that played off of DITD's pitch dark exhibitions.

Takeaway

Learnings

In hindsight, I've realized this campaign was my first experience using human-centered design thinking. The sensitive yet important issues we worked with led us to focus on how the user will interact with our ideas and message.

A concern that came up at times as we iterated our designs was whether viewers "would get it?". Was this too simple? Are we saying enough? But as we shared our ideas with our peers and mentor, these worries dissipated. I realized that sometimes people like to think, and tapping into their curiosity is sometimes more powerful than spelling things out.
