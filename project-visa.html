Working off the foundations set by our partner teams at AKQA and Visa HQ, our Visa Team at BBDO was tasked with updating Visa’s Creative Playbook. In particular, I was tasked with refining layout guidelines for Visa’s iconic navy and gold flag, setting design standards for Visa’s many partnered brands and events, and exploring new ways Visa could implement illustrations in their branding. All these tasks set the guidelines for many of the designs I had the opportunity to create while on the Visa Creative Team, from simple banner ads to branded messaging displayed at the Rio 2016 Olympics.

With each project during my time on BBDO’s Visa Creative Team, I generally used the following process. I gathered new data and research specific to each brief, but also relied on the brand knowledge and user data I had accumulated in previous Visa projects. I considered the guidelines we worked on in the Creative playbook to be a part of the ideating process and the launched projects were designed implementing these standards.

Brand Needs

Everywhere you want to be

Wherever you want to be Visa helps you get there. In all messaging our client wanted to convey that Visa is always with you, helping you get to where you want to be, whether that be a physical place or simply a state of mind. Especially with the 2016 Rio Olympics coming up, Visa wanted to ensure their creative guidelines were consistent so consumers of all backgrounds could feel secure and empowered in Visa.

Consistency

Every project Visa put its name on utilized different media types from traditional to interactive. The way Visa’s iconic flag could be incorporated needed more guidelines to ensure they conveyed Visa’s brand values.

Sponsors and partnerships

As a financial service Visa rarely works alone. Partnering with other companies frequent and constant, guidelines for how logos and branding were to play nicely with others was needed. The design guides needed to accommodate for factors that. For this task we were specifically trying to reduce pain points for other designers working with Visa.

Exploring Illustration

Illustration icons and animations help add personality and friendliness to a brand. Visa tasked us with exploring options in line with the new guidelines.

Learnings

Wrapping things up

In our increasingly cashless world, Visa is a brand as integrated into everyday life as possible. While initially both daunting and exhilarating to be putting my hands on work with such a wide audience, I came out of the experience on the Visa team with a clear understanding of the need to reach users in familiar yet exciting ways. I was often put to the test by working with a well-established brand to create new experiences without moving off brand.

Working on a brand with a reach as wide as Visa’s gave me the opportunity to work across a wide-breadth of mediums and with many different clients. A consistent design process was essential to staying on top of the constant flow of work. Not all clients I work for will be as large as Visa, but the attention to detail and rapid execution of carefully thought-out design decisions can be applied to all future projects.
